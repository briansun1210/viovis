Introduction

Hi, we are Luke Gamelin, Thai On, and Brian Sun from University of Massachusetts, Amherst.

Let our visualizations tell you the story. A story that has been of great interest. A story that has brought a major movement to human races. A story that has brought attention to the need of understanding between people and police.

Our purpose is to bring knowledge to the matter around police killing people during the year of 2013 to 2021. Here, we provide an overview, with a slight focus on the Black population in particular. We let our data speak the truth. Please keep in mind that we appreciate the job that the police have been contributing, and treasure the lives of people of every color. We keep our tone as data reporters and visualizers as professional and neutral as possible.

With that being said, we, the data visualizers, hope you have a satisfactory and educational experience visiting our website.

By States/By Police Departments

The US map itself is a heat map area for the percentage of police killings over population in each state. On the left hand side of the map, there is a stacked bar chart indicating the heat color based on the police killing as a percentage of population. This US map includes 4 more additional visualizations. (Note: Please choose one in the drop down menu, then hover to each state to see the data) Firstly, there is a spider graph for armed vs unarmed, and vehicle vs unclear on people being encountered. Secondly, it's the bar charts for top 10 city with number of black people killed in each state Thirdly, it's the Venn Diagram for the number of Black population, Number of black people killed, and Number of police killing causes. Fourthly, it's the table chart for annual police killings from each police department. (Note: Please to hover on the each dot to see the table chart)

Node link diagram showing encounter types, races, and causes of death

There are various encounter tyes, and causes of death of the people by the police. This node link graph is displaying the relationship between these scenarios among different races, and the number of cases in each category.
